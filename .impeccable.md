## Design Context

### Users
Treasury team at SBPL (commodity trading, West Africa/China trade) — finance professionals at their desks, daily operators who live in this tool alongside spreadsheets and bank portals. Management/CFO also reviews occasionally. Both audiences need clarity fast; neither tolerates decoration for its own sake.

### Brand Personality
Clean · Professional · Current — in the register of Mercury, Brex, or Stripe. Functional but polished, slightly warmer than Bloomberg-institutional. Not decorative.

### Aesthetic Direction
Sharpen the existing design language without overhauling it. The minimal, neutral, data-dense approach is right. Improve font choice, fix the type scale, and add more contrast between hierarchy levels. Light mode. No dark theme needed.

### Design Principles
1. **Data density is a feature** — Small type (11–13px) is appropriate for financial dashboards. Every label earns its space.
2. **Hierarchy through contrast** — Use size AND weight AND color together; don't rely on just one dimension.
3. **Numbers deserve care** — Tabular nums, monospace stacks, aligned decimals. Financial data needs typographic precision.
4. **Restraint over decoration** — Nothing added that doesn't serve the data. No ornamental borders, no gradients.
5. **Functional trust** — The UI should feel built by people who understand finance, not a marketing team.
